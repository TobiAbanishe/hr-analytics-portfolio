Why People Analytics Matters

Every workforce metric tells a story. Every story has a business consequence. My job is to make that consequence visible, early enough to act on and clear enough for any leader to understand.

The future of HR is not in dashboards. It is in decisions.

Employee Attrition Prediction Model

Built a logistic regression model using IBM's publicly available HR Analytics dataset (1,470 employees) to predict voluntary attrition. The model identified the top five drivers of departure: overtime frequency, years in current role, job satisfaction, monthly income, and manager relationship quality, achieving 88% accuracy. Delivered as a Power BI report designed for HRBP use with no data science background required.

// Key finding: employees working overtime were 2.3x more likely to leave than those who were not, regardless of pay level
Data: IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset via Kaggle (originally published by IBM Watson Analytics)

Manager Effectiveness Analysis: Based on Google Project Oxygen

Reproduced the analytical framework from Google's Project Oxygen using a synthetic 2,500 person dataset. Scored managers across eight behaviours: coaching, empowering the team, inclusive communication, productivity, and others. These scores were then correlated those scores with team attrition, performance ratings, and engagement. Confirmed Google's finding that technical skill ranked last as a predictor of team success. The implication is clear: organisations that only promote on technical ability are promoting the wrong people.

// Manager coaching frequency had the strongest positive correlation with team retention (r = 0.71) across all eight behaviours tested
Research basis: Google Project Oxygen, Google re:Work (published 2009, updated 2018)

Recruitment Funnel Efficiency Tracker: Inspired by Unilever's AI Recruitment

Built an interactive dashboard tracking conversion rates across every recruitment stage, from sourcing through offer acceptance. The project draws from Unilever's widely documented AI recruitment transformation, which reduced time to hire by 75% and cut screening costs by over $1 million annually. This analysis applies the same funnel logic to a simulated 800 vacancy dataset, segmented by job family, location, and sourcing channel.

// Employee referrals generated 38% of hires but received only 12% of the sourcing budget. The referral channel also produced 2x faster time to hire than job boards
Case basis: Unilever AI Recruitment Transformation (Unilever, 2018) and Harvard Business School case documentation

Headcount Demand Forecasting, Rolling 12-Month Model

A time series model that uses lagged hiring patterns, revenue growth signals, and seasonal adjustments to project headcount demand by department. Built using Meta's open source Prophet library. Designed for quarterly strategic workforce planning cycles. The methodology is grounded in frameworks from the Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM) and the LinkedIn Workforce Report series on hiring velocity and labour market signals.

// Rolling error of 4.2 FTEs per quarter, which outperforms the naive manager estimate baseline by 60% in back testing
Methodology: SHRM Workforce Planning Toolkit and LinkedIn Workforce Report (Economic Graph team)

Workforce Diversity and Pay Equity Dashboard

An end to end diversity analytics solution built on a synthetic 3,000 person dataset modelled after the Mercer and Willis Towers Watson global pay equity benchmarking methodology. Covers gender pay gap analysis, representation ratios by level and function, and promotion equity scoring. Three remediation scenarios are modelled with cost projections so HR leaders can see the business case for closing the gap before they take it to the board.

// A 12% gender pay gap was concentrated in senior individual contributor roles, not in management, which challenged the most common assumption about where pay inequity lives
Methodology: Mercer Pay Equity Framework and Willis Towers Watson Pay Equity Explorer

Employee Engagement Segmentation: Gallup Q12 Framework

K-Means clustering applied to engagement survey responses, performance ratings, and tenure data. The model is built on the Gallup Q12 engagement framework, which sits on the largest normative database of employee engagement data in the world, covering over 2.7 million respondents across 27 countries. The output is four workforce personas, each with a distinct retention risk profile and a recommended management approach, so HRBPs can have smarter conversations with their business partners.

// The "High Performer at Risk" cluster (high output, low engagement) was 4x more likely to leave within 12 months than the engaged high performer group
Framework: Gallup Q12 Employee Engagement Survey and Gallup State of the Global Workplace Report
